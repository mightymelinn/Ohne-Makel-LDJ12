This is a short 3D top-down turn-based strategy level created within a 5-week game jam, where the player navigate heavy mecha nodes through an abandoned cathedral to destroy corrupted spirits and rescue captured souls.

## About Me & This Repository
Whatsup homie! I'm Melinn 👋, a game designer currently based in Vancouver, BC. It's that jam time of the year! You know I never miss it. But this time, I’m challenging myself with 3 new rules for this jam:

1. **The Design Roulette**: I wanted to force myself out of my comfort zone, so I tied genres, settings, and themes to random numbers, and had my human RNG blindly pick what I will create for the next month. You could say I'm *definitely* out of my comfort zone right now.
2. **Tracked Iteration**: I want to see exactly how many times I iterate from paper design to the final playable level. I'll be counting and updating my progress in the devlog here!
3. **No Crunching!**: A promise to myself! I am keeping the scope incredibly tight so I can focus on having a highly playtestable build. 

I made this repo public to share my raw, behind-the-scenes progress :3 - and if you spot a cool way to optimize my BPs, I'm always down to learn!

## My Design Goals
Since Turn-Based Strategy is arguably one of the hardest genres to execute in a single month, I need to be ruthless with my scope. I am avoiding creating new complex abilities and will instead utilize the core mechanics already available in the game template. The entire focus will heavily rely on **designing the map geometry to create variety and challenge.**

### 1. Topology
To interpret **"The City"** theme within an abandoned cathedral, I want the layout of the map itself to be the main puzzle. Instead of a flat combat board, my goal is to use the architectural geometry of the grid to create natural chokepoints, block line-of-sight, and force the player to think critically about their spatial positioning.

### 2. Enemy Encounter
This is what I'm most excited about! I want to focusing on how enemy placement can naturally force the player into tactical decisions. I want to experiment with how different enemy setups force the player to constantly weigh their options and prioritize their targets. I just have to get into the editor and start building so I can try things out and vent out my ideas!

### 3. Combat Pacing
I want to build a distinct pacing arc for the encounters. If the player formulates a master plan on Turn 1, my goal as the level designer is to break it. To achieve this while keeping the scope tight, I am allowing myself to program only *one* major environmental hazard to disrupt the player's rhythm. If it threatens the timeline, I am completely willing to cut it.
